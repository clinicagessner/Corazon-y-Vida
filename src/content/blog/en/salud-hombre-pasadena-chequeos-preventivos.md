# Men's Health in Pasadena: Preventive Checkups That Can Save Your Life

Many Hispanic men in Pasadena and southeast Houston only see a doctor when the pain no longer lets them work. Between work, family, and responsibilities, their own health always comes last. The problem is that conditions like high blood pressure, diabetes, and prostate problems progress silently for years, with no symptoms, until serious complications appear.

The good news: a simple preventive checkup, once or twice a year, can catch these problems early, when they are still easy to control.

## Why Do Regular Checkups Matter?

Preventive exams allow you to:

- Detect high blood pressure, diabetes, and high cholesterol before they damage your heart, kidneys, or vision
- Monitor prostate health starting at the recommended age
- Identify low testosterone levels that cause fatigue, low mood, and lack of energy
- Find infections that show no symptoms, including some sexually transmitted infections
- Know your baseline numbers (glucose, cholesterol, blood pressure) so changes are caught early

Catching a disease early almost always means simpler, more affordable treatment with better results.

## Our Men's Health Services

### Prostate Exam (PSA)

The prostate-specific antigen (PSA) is a blood test that helps monitor prostate health. In general, it's recommended to discuss this test with your doctor starting at age 50, or earlier if you have a family history of prostate cancer.

### Testosterone Panel

If you feel tired all the time, low on energy, discouraged, or notice changes in your intimate life, low testosterone may be the cause. A simple blood test gives you the answer, and our [men's health services](/services/salud-hombre) include testing with results explained in Spanish.

### Complete Blood Work

Our [blood testing laboratory](/services/examenes-sangre) evaluates glucose, cholesterol, triglycerides, kidney and liver function, and more. It's the foundation of any preventive checkup.

### Electrocardiogram

An [electrocardiogram](/services/electrocardiograma) checks your heart's rhythm and electrical activity. It's fast, painless, and helps detect heart problems before they become emergencies.

## Warning Signs: Don't Ignore Them

Come to the clinic as soon as possible if you experience:

- Chest pain or pressure, especially with exertion
- Difficulty urinating, weak stream, or getting up several times at night
- Excessive thirst, constant hunger, and frequent urination
- Persistent headaches with blurred vision
- Swelling in your legs, ankles, or feet
- Changes in erectile function (they can be an early sign of circulation problems)
- Lumps or changes in the testicles

## Practical Tips to Take Care of Your Health

1. **Get an annual checkup** even if you feel fine — that's how silent diseases work
2. **Know your numbers:** blood pressure, glucose, and cholesterol
3. **Move 30 minutes a day:** walking counts
4. **Cut back on sodas and fried food**, especially if diabetes runs in your family
5. **Limit alcohol and avoid smoking**
6. **Sleep 7-8 hours:** poor sleep raises blood pressure and blood sugar
7. **Don't self-medicate** for pain or infections: it can hide a bigger problem
